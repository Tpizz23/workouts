Legs + Shoulders + Core
Build size with purpose today — strong compounds first, then clean hypertrophy work to finish the target muscles.
Equipment: Full-GymDuration: 45–60 minGoal: Bulk · Chest priorityStart: 3:30 PM
Warm-Up
  • 5 minutes on the bike plus bodyweight squats, arm circles, and band pull-aparts for 2 rounds.
  • 1 ramp-up set each for back squats and shoulder presses before the first work sets.
  • 10 glute bridges and 10 dead bugs per side to wake up hips and trunk.
Workout

Legs

Barbell Back Squat

Unrack the bar, sit down between the hips, and drive up through the whole foot.

4 sets6–8 reps

Primary compound — heavy

Leg Press

Lower the sled under control and press through mid-foot to train quads and glutes hard.

3 sets10–12 reps

High foot placement for glutes/hams

Romanian Deadlift

Hinge at the hips with a soft knee bend and drive the hips through to stand tall.

3 sets10–12 reps

Hip hinge, slight knee bend

Leg Curl (lying or seated)

Curl the pad toward the glutes and lower it slowly to fully train the hamstrings.

3 sets12 reps

Full extension, controlled

Shoulders

Machine Shoulder Press

Press the handles overhead on a fixed path and control the return to shoulder level.

3 sets10–12 reps

Burnout/finisher

Cable Lateral Raise

Raise the handle out to the side against constant cable tension for clean delt work.

3 sets15 reps

Constant cable tension

DB Rear Delt Fly

Hinge forward and sweep the arms wide to target the rear shoulders.

3 sets12–15 reps

Bent over or on incline bench face-down

Core

Cable Crunch (kneeling)

Crunch the ribcage down toward the hips while keeping the hips mostly still.

3 sets15 reps

Crunch down, not pull

Hanging Leg Raise

Lift the legs from a dead hang without swinging to hit the lower abs and hip flexors.

3 sets12–15 repsBodyweight

Strict, no swing

Cool-Down / Stretch
  • Couch stretch and hamstring stretch for 30 seconds per side.
  • Door-frame pec stretch and cross-body shoulder stretch for 30 seconds per side.
  • Slow breathing in child’s pose for 60 seconds to bring the heart rate down.
