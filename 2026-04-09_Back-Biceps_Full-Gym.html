🔙 Back + Biceps

Thursday, April 9, 2026  ·  Full-Gym
🏋️ Full-Gym ⏱ 45–60 min 🎯 Bulk: 165 → 180 lb
🔥 Warm-Up (5 min)
  • Cat-cow and thoracic spine rotations — 1 min, wake up the entire back chain
  • Band pull-aparts × 10 — rear delt and rhomboid activation
  • Light pull-up or inverted row hold × 5 — lat activation under load
  • Light barbell bent-over row × 8 with just the bar — spinal Erector warm-up
  • Face pulls × 10 with light cable — rear delt and rotator cuff activation
🏋️ Workout
BACK (5 exercises)
Pull-Up or Weighted Pull-Up
Hang from a bar with an overhand grip just outside shoulder width, then pull yourself up until your chin clears the bar and lower with control.
4 sets 6–10 reps
Primary compound for back — full dead hang to chin over the bar. Add weight with a dip belt when you can hit 10 reps easily. Deader hang at the bottom for 1 second.
Weight (lb):
Barbell Bent-Over Row
Hinge forward at the hips to about 45 degrees, then pull the barbell into your lower ribcage by driving your elbows back and squeezing your shoulder blades together.
4 sets 8–10 reps
Overhand grip, chest up, and a flat lower back. Big pull — think about dragging the bar toward your belly button.
Weight (lb):
Seated Cable Row (V-bar)
Sit at a cable row station with a V-bar, plant your feet, and pull the handle toward your lower abdomen while keeping your torso upright and squeezing your shoulder blades back.
3 sets 10–12 reps
Drive your elbows back past your torso and squeeze the shoulder blades together at the end of each rep. Full stretch at the arms extended position.
Lat Pulldown (wide grip)
Sit at the lat pulldown machine, grip the bar wide, lean back slightly, and pull the bar down to your upper chest while driving your elbows down and back.
3 sets 10–12 reps
Think about leading with your elbows rather than your hands. Squeeze hard at the bottom for 1 second. Use this when pull-ups are adequately loaded.
Face Pull (cable, rope)
With a rope attachment on a high cable, pull the rope toward your face by separating your hands and driving your elbows back, finishing with the rope ends beside your ears.
3 sets 15 reps
External rotation at the top — this protects your shoulder joint and builds the rear delts. Slow and controlled, no jerking.
BICEPS (4 exercises)
Barbell Curl
Stand with a barbell at arm's length and curl it up to shoulder height by contracting your biceps, keeping your elbows pinned at your sides and your wrists neutral.
3 sets 10–12 reps
Strict standing form — no swinging or body English. Squeeze hard at the top for 1 full second before lowering slowly.
Weight (lb):
Incline DB Curl
Set an adjustable bench to about 45–60 degrees, lie back with arms hanging straight down, and curl the dumbbells up to shoulder height.
3 sets 12 reps
The incline stretch hits the long head of the bicep hard — do not skip the eccentric portion. Lower slowly for 2 seconds.
Weight (lb):
Cable Curl (straight bar)
Stand facing a low cable with a straight bar attachment and curl the bar up to shoulder height, keeping your elbows stationary at your sides throughout.
3 sets 12–15 reps
Constant cable tension means the bicep is under load throughout the entire range — this is especially effective at the bottom of the movement.
Preacher Curl (EZ bar or machine)
Sit at a preacher bench with your arms resting on the pad, and curl the weight up by contracting your biceps without letting your arms fully extend at the bottom.
2 sets 12 reps
Pure isolation — no cheating or momentum. Full stretch at the bottom and a full contraction at the top. Works the short head of the bicep.

❄️ Cool-Down / Stretch (2 min)
  • Doorframe lat stretch — arms at 90° in a doorframe, lean through for 30 sec per side
  • Child's pose with arms extended — hold 30 sec, focus on lat expansion
  • Standing bicep stretch — pull one arm across chest with the opposite hand, 30 sec/side
  • Foam roll upper back — roll slowly through the thoracic spine for 1 min
  • 5 deep, slow breaths — exhale fully to activate the parasympathetic system
