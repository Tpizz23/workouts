Chest + Triceps
Build size with purpose today — strong compounds first, then clean hypertrophy work to finish the target muscles.
Equipment: Limited-BarbellDuration: 45–60 minGoal: Bulk · Chest priorityStart: 2:30 PM
Warm-Up
  • 2 rounds of arm circles, band-free shoulder openers, and 10 incline push-ups to wake up the pressing muscles.
  • 1 light set each of DB floor press and DB floor fly before the first working set.
  • Scapular push-ups for 30 seconds to lock in shoulder position.
Workout

Chest

DB Floor Press

Drive the dumbbells from the floor to lockout while keeping your upper arms controlled against the ground.

4 sets8–10 reps

Primary compound — go heavy

Wide Push-Up

Press from a wide hand position to load the chest through a long bodyweight range of motion.

3 sets12–15 repsBodyweight

Bodyweight chest width

DB Floor Fly

Open the arms in a hugging arc and squeeze the pecs as the bells come back together.

3 sets10–12 reps

Controlled stretch at bottom

Incline Push-Up (feet elevated)

Use an elevated foot position to bias the upper chest while staying rigid through the torso.

3 sets10–15 repsBodyweight

Upper chest emphasis

DB Pullover (floor)

Lower one dumbbell behind the head and pull it back over the chest to train pecs and serratus.

3 sets12 reps

Chest/serratus stretch

Triceps

DB Skull Crusher (floor)

Bend at the elbows to lower the dumbbells toward the forehead, then extend hard without flaring.

3 sets10–12 reps

Light-moderate weight, elbows in

DB Overhead Tricep Extension

Lower the dumbbell behind the head and straighten the elbows to train the long head of the triceps.

3 sets12–15 reps

Single or double arm

Close-Grip Push-Up

Keep the hands narrow and elbows tucked so the triceps drive most of the pressing work.

3 sets10–15 repsBodyweight

Elbows tight to body

Cool-Down / Stretch
  • Doorway chest stretch for 30 seconds per side.
  • Overhead triceps stretch and wrist flexor stretch for 30 seconds each.
  • Slow nasal breathing while lying on the floor for 60 seconds to bring heart rate down.
